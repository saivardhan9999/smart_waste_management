Literature Review

1. Introduction

Waste management has emerged as a critical challenge in modern society, with increasing urbanization and industrial growth generating massive amounts of solid waste. Traditional waste management systems rely heavily on manual sorting, which is labor-intensive, time-consuming, and prone to human error. The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Computer Vision technologies presents a transformative solution to automate and optimize waste classification processes.

2. Related Work

Deep Learning in Waste Classification

Wang et al. (2020) proposed a ResNet-based waste classification system achieving 85% accuracy on a 5-class dataset. Their approach demonstrated the effectiveness of transfer learning in waste categorization tasks.

Yang et al. (2021) developed a hybrid CNN-RNN architecture for waste classification, leveraging both spatial and temporal features to improve classification accuracy to 88% on diverse waste materials.

Explainable AI in Environmental Applications

Zhang et al. (2022) introduced Grad-CAM visualizations in waste classification systems to improve transparency and trust in AI decisions. Their study highlighted the importance of explainability in deployment scenarios.

MobileNet and Edge Deployment

Howard et al. (2019) developed MobileNetV2, a lightweight CNN architecture optimized for mobile and embedded devices. The architecture balances accuracy and computational efficiency, making it ideal for real-time waste detection systems.
